Custom 64-bit Verilog Calculator

Chip Design • Digital • Circuit Design

I completed this project as part of the digital onboarding for a chip design club at my school. There were three phases, and we were given two weeks to complete each one. The first part was creating a 64-bit calculator capable of performing unsigned integer addtion using systemVerilog, which had to pass testing. Stage two was verification, and stage three was physical design.

Going into this project, I had literally never heard of a hardware description language before, so the learning curve was definitely steep. My main resources were a handy site called HDLbits, the vast wide web, and my CompE friends who actually knew what they were doing. Much to my suprise (and likely the suprise of the team leads), I was able to finish phase one right before the deadline! During phase two, I gained skills in verification software, Xcelium, and coverage analysis. However, I managed my time a bit more poorly for this phase, and ended up working on the last stages right after completing my first ever hackathon, hackGT (read about my submission, HealthDoc, in the projects page!). Sleep deprived from pulling two consecutive near all nighters, I accidentally tied together read and write ports... whoops. This issue took me a whooping 3 hours to debug, and I ended up turning in my project at 12:03 AM... just three minutes after the deadline. As a result, I didn't get to attempt the physical design stage. However, I can still confidently say I grew a lot as a result of this project.

First off, this was the most self-learning I had done in a while, and I was pleasantly suprised with how quickly I picked up the new topic. I also learned how to utilize my resources to learn quickly and adapt, including the amazing resource that was my social network! Seriously, my friends are wicked smart and I'm so grateful for their guideance (shoutout to Fiona Li for helping me realize I tied together my ports lol). I also realized that, while I had fun, I was more interested in analog, and physics heavy projects, than digital. As a result, I made the decision to attempt the analog onboarding the following semester. This time, I was successful! Read about it in the projects tab :)
